Produce a single SETTING REFERENCE SHEET illustration in vibrant watercolor style for a children's picture book. This image will anchor every page of a friendly deep-space adventure — it is the visual "likeness" of the cosmos this story takes place in.

NO human characters. NO animals. NO story creatures (no meteor, no moon sheep, no robot, no alien — those arrive on later pages). Just the environment and its recurring cosmic landmarks, clearly visible.

Scene: A warm, full, friendly stretch of cosmos viewed in "open picture-book" deep space, three-quarter angle that shows a full spread of planets, comet, nebula, and star fields. The cosmos is warm and inhabited, never cold empty void.

Required celestial elements (render clearly and memorably):
- A big warm YELLOW MOON at CENTER with a gentle smiling face and soft crater freckles, butter-gold, the visual anchor of the cosmos
- A turquoise RINGED PLANET at LOWER-LEFT, pale mint-turquoise body with soft pale-mint rings tilted slightly, glowing gently from within
- A candy-striped red-and-white COMET at UPPER-RIGHT with a soft sparkle tail arcing behind it
- Three golden STARS in a small triangle at MIDDLE-RIGHT, warm gold, clearly grouped
- A rosy-pink NEBULA cloud puffing softly across the UPPER-LEFT background like cotton candy, pink and peach

Required recurring props (render each exactly once, in specific positions that will repeat across all pages):
- A gentle scatter of tiny silver STARS dusted throughout the whole frame, pinpricks of warm white-silver
- A soft ribbon hint of silver-pink-gold STARDUST curling faintly through the mid-distance (a whisper of the singing stardust that will return later)
- A soft smiling expression on the moon that is unmistakable but calm — eyes closed-crescent, small warm curve of a mouth

Environment details (soft, lived-in):
- A faint drift of rosy nebula dust curling in from upper-left toward the moon
- One tiny silver bubble-star flickering near the comet's tail
- A gentle pink-and-gold glow around the ringed planet's rings where the moon's light catches them

Lighting: warm friendly starlight — the moon throws a soft butter-gold glow outward from center, the nebula adds a rosy-pink wash from upper-left, the ringed planet adds a gentle turquoise rim-glow from lower-left. No cold empty black; space here is warm and full, roughly a mixed warm palette near 4000K.

Art style: modern vibrant watercolor illustration. Rich saturated colors, confident playful shapes, soft paper grain, contemporary picture-book energy — think Oliver Jeffers, Sam Usher, Christian Robinson at their most vivid. NOT muted, NOT vintage, NOT sepia. Bright and joyful. Soft edges, painterly, no harsh black outlines.

Composition: wide horizontal framing, smiling yellow moon as the clear center anchor, ringed planet lower-left, comet upper-right, golden star triangle middle-right, rosy nebula upper-left, silver stars dusted everywhere. Plenty of open cosmos around the moon so future page illustrations have room for characters and creatures to float through this same anchored stage.

Exclusions (critical): no text, no letters, no numbers, no labels, no watermarks, no signatures, no borders, no frames, no panels, no humans, no animals, no rocket (the rocket appears as a moving prop on the pages, not here), no meteor face, no moon sheep, no robot, no alien, no cold blue-black void, no scary dark empty space.
