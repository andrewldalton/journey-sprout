Produce a single SETTING REFERENCE SHEET illustration in warm watercolor style for a children's picture book. This image anchors the enchanted nighttime forest that appears on pages 3–9 of a night-story — the visual "likeness" of the magical woods.

NO human characters. NO animals. Just the environment and its recurring magical props, clearly visible.

Scene: A wide establishing view of a warm enchanted nighttime forest, from the edge of a dew-beaded garden looking in, with the forest's key landmarks visible across the frame so this reference captures every spot the story visits.

Required environment elements (render clearly and memorably across the frame):
- Dewy grass foreground in the very front, low curl of warm forest mist along the ground
- A small crooked garden path of three flat pale stones leading from the near left into the tree line
- A low flat stone garden step just in the left foreground (the boundary between home and forest)
- Tall silhouetted pines and birches ringing the middle ground, warm umber trunks, deep forest green canopy
- Waist-high ferns curling at the base of the trees
- A mossy fallen log resting across the midground, cushion of green moss along its top
- The enormous gnarled base of an ancient tree in the middle of the frame, rising out of the ground with thick roots

Required recurring magical props (render each exactly once, so their look is locked for every page that features them):
- A cluster of five butter-gold glowing mushrooms at varied heights growing at the base of the gnarled tree root — warm golden radiance, not yellow-white
- A low arching tree branch hanging above the clearing, hung with three tulip-shaped LANTERN FLOWERS at three stages: closed bud (far left), fully open glowing silver-warm middle, half-wilted (far right)

Lighting: warm night. The forest itself holds deep umber browns and deep forest greens. The glowing mushrooms and the lantern flowers and the moonbeam in the web provide the only light sources — warm butter-gold, warm silver, warm honey. Art style: warm watercolor with digital polish, soft edges, visible paper grain, rich warm palette, soft blurred distant trees with sharper foreground props, no harsh black outlines, no line-art.

Composition: wide horizontal establishing framing so all the anchor landmarks (mushroom cluster, lantern flower branch, spiderweb between birches, moss ring) are visible in one reference — page illustrations will zoom in on each of these in turn. Strong warm glows from the magical props pull the eye inward. Plenty of ground and midground space around each landmark so character action has room.

Exclusions (critical): no text, no letters, no numbers, no labels, no watermarks, no signatures, no borders, no frames, no panels, no humans, no animals besides the one distant moth, no daylight, no stars in the sky (the first stars arrive during the story), no full moon disc.
