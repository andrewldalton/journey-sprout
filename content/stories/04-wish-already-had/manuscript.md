## Emotional arc (3 sentences)

A child at dusk, safe among beloved things, is offered a wish by a soft-spoken companion and reaches — as children do — for bigger, shinier, more. As the wish begins to take, the ordinary treasures around them start to thin and fade, and the child feels the cold shape of losing what was already theirs. They rush to gather what is real, hold it tight, and in choosing it back the world returns — not the same, but more, because now they can see it.

Across pages, these things CAN change per brief: time-of-day tilting from early dusk into twilight into soft glowing night; the rising and lighting of fireflies from ankle-high (page 1) to full column (page 9); the soft-gold shimmer that begins pooling around the companion on page 4 and settles into a lamp-warmth by page 10; and — critically — the saturation and solidity of the three locked objects, which visibly thin and fade on pages 5–7 (paper-canvas showing through) and restore on pages 8–10. The clearing geometry, tree ring, birch-trunk emergence point, and the identity of the bear/dog/blanket are LOCKED.
