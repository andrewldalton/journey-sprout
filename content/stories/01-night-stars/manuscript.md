## Emotional arc (3 sentences)

The child steps out into a hush of twilight and finds a tiny rocket waiting with their companion, and a whispered "ready?" turns into a real and rumbling YES. Page by page the sky opens wider — a pinch of stardust, a polite meteor, a giggling moon sheep, a joke-collecting robot, a baby alien waving with all six hands — and what felt big and unknown turns out to be kind and curious and full of friends. By the time they loop home through a starry sky, the child has learned the very best thing about the universe: it is enormous, and it is friendly, and going anyway is where all the wonder lives.
